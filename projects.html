Projects

Educational Workshops on Land Care

We provide weekly educational workshops for all age groups in different areas, where people can gain both theoretical and practical knowledge in their interested area. The topics include land conservation, bushfire revegetation, increasing biodiversity, creating an ecosystem for wildlife, as well as learning about the Aboriginal culture and their land care practices.

Private Land Conservation

We acknowledge that fact that most of NSW is owned by private land owners so we partner with numerous land owners to care for our land and create a better and healthier future together. We create better ecosystems and biodiversity in NSW through planting a diverse range of native plants, which then creates habitat for wildlife.

Bushfire Revegetation

Australia experiences bushfires every summer due to the lack of rain throughout the year followed by the extreme temperatures. During this time, many ecosystems are disturbed, as well as forests and lands are damaged. So we put a great attention to helping these areas of NSW to be restored to their original self as quick as possible.

Aboriginal Community Engagement

Our organisation puts a great emphasis on creating deeper connections with Aboriginal communities as they are the traditional custodians of the land that we now live on. This involves partnerships with Aboriginal communities to collaborate together on Landcare activities. Our aim is to increase recognition of the Aboriginal culture by incoporating cultural protocols and Aboriginal perspectives into our projects.
